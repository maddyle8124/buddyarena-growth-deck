TL;DR

The plan in three answers.

01 · Bring users in
Start warm, not cold

No user base, no ad budget — so we don't chase strangers. We start with people our partners can already reach (Yellow.Pro first, Aster next) and build the product so traders bring their friends. Three simple channels seed the first group. Target: 50 real, active traders — not a big signup number.

02 · Keep them
Build for the comeback

One trader who returns every week is worth more than eight who try once. Weekly rounds (epochs) build the habit. Three features Cede Hub doesn't have — Telegram reminders, a live leaderboard anyone can watch without logging in, and a last-hour bonus — pull traders back and put us in front of their friends at the same time.

03 · Grow from there
Each campaign sells the next

Traders create volume. That volume — real and provable — is what convinces the next client to fund a prize pool. Every finished campaign becomes a case study; every case study wins the next client; every new client brings their own community of traders. That's the flywheel.

TL;DR
Section 1 · The Strategic Bet

Start from where we really are — said honestly.

This isn't a launch from strength. Naming the limits plainly is the only way to build a plan that survives the real world.

Where we actually stand

We only track trades — the app watches volume on Binance/MEXC, it can't trade for anyone. No bot, no vouchers → the prize pool is the only reason to join.
No users yet — every early trader must come through someone else's network.
No exchange deal yet — we haven't applied for affiliate programs; the "easy money" campaigns can't run yet.
Small, slow budget — ~$2K pool to start; Yellow decision-makers move slowly.

The bet: make every touchpoint count

Since we can't pull strangers in at scale, we do the opposite: bring people in through warm partner networks, polish every step they take inside the product, keep them coming back — and make sharing so easy they spread us themselves.

We don't have an audience. We have a partner with an audience — and a product built to make that audience share itself.

Will not

Run paid ads

No budget — and paid traffic to an unknown product converts into nothing.

Will not

Fake the volume

Bot or wash volume would destroy partner trust for good. If a pool underperforms, we extend the window — we never fake activity.

Will not

Spam channels

Mass-dropping links gets us banned and looks desperate. We go through warm, partner-backed intros instead.

The pool problem, said out loud

Split $2K across ~8 weekly winners and each prize is $50–150. That's real money for an active retail trader — but nothing for a whale. So at $2K we aim for active retail traders, and we push for a $5–10K pool as a condition for launch: below a believable minimum, the campaign isn't worth running.

The sharing loop — and its honest limit

top trader earns → shares their card → a friend joins → volume goes up → the pool looks bigger → more people compete harder → more sharing.

With only 50 traders, sharing won't flood us with new users yet — what it does is show outsiders the competition is real. It becomes a true growth driver in Phase 2–3, once there are enough traders sharing. We build it now because it's cheap and it compounds.

The catch, and how we handle it: exchange commission can't fund a pool until volume is high — but volume needs a pool first. So we seed the first easy-money rounds from a small float or leftover partner budget; commission then refills the next round. Commission never pays for the first one. A cross-campaign bonus (join 2+ campaigns → earn extra) then moves these traders into the partner campaigns clients pay for.

Personal "you're 4th, 5h left" messages — using the Telegram login we already have. Nothing new to build.

A public link anyone can open without an account — "watch me compete live." The leaderboard becomes the ad.

Every round ends with a sprint instead of fading out. Drama people share.

If early weeks look thin (the same few names winning small amounts), switch to bi-weekly rounds so each winner post has more substance. Community: a Telegram announce channel (no moderation needed) + a chat group — promote a top trader to moderator at ~100 members.

The "Partners" page — an easy front door for clients

Trading Competition — a ready-made campaign with weekly rounds and all the retention features. Built already.
Trading Volume Provider — open, contracted market-making using Buddy Trading's existing bot engine, for projects that must hold a volume level to keep their exchange listing.

The line we draw — said before anyone asks

The volume-provider service is openly disclosed market-making for the client's own token — and it stays completely separate from competition leaderboards. Competition volume is always from real traders, measured on our servers, never from bots. We never sell the two as the same thing. When a client asks "your competitions are real but you also sell bot volume?" — the answer is already written on the page.

If our first number is small: we tell the value-for-money story — how much real volume each prize dollar bought, what one verified trader cost — instead of a size story we'd lose against Cede Hub's $1B+.

Section 10 · KPIs & Open Decisions

Measure what sells the next client — not what looks good.

We're not pouring everything into user acquisition — we don't have the network for it. We make every touchpoint work, keep traders coming back, and let them advocate for us. If Phase 1 ends with one trustworthy volume number and traders who came back, it worked — even if the follower count never moved.

We measure

Real volume (the number X) — one defensible USDT figure, measured on our servers, above a minimum we set before launch.
50 genuine active traders — "active" = real counted volume in at least one weekly round.
Comebacks — traders active in one round who return for the next.
Posting discipline — did the full posting sequence run every round.

We don't measure

Raw signups — a signup that never trades proves nothing.
Follower counts — posting is for trust, not a scoreboard.
Easy-money campaign numbers — that engine belongs to Phase 2.
