Section 1 · The Strategic Bet

Where we stand — said honestly.

The constraints

We only track trades — no bot, no vouchers. The prize pool is the only reason to join.
No users yet — every early trader comes through someone else's network.
No exchange deal yet — affiliate applications not filed.
Small, slow budget — ~$2K pool to start; Yellow moves slowly.

The bet: make every touchpoint count

Bring people in through warm partner networks, polish every step inside the product, keep them coming back — and make sharing so easy they spread us themselves.

We don't have an audience. We have a partner with an audience — and a product built to make that audience share itself.

Will not

Run paid ads

No budget; cold traffic to an unknown product converts into nothing.

Will not

Fake the volume

Would destroy partner trust for good. Underperforming pool → extend the window.

Will not

Spam channels

Gets us banned and looks desperate. Warm, partner-backed intros only.

The pool problem: $2K across ~8 weekly winners = $50–150 per prize. Real money for an active retail trader, nothing for a whale. So at $2K we target retail — and we push for $5–10K as a condition for launch.

Section 12 · Proposed Model — For Discussion

Self-run "house campaigns" — our shelf is never empty.

Today a campaign only exists when a B2B partner funds one. "Always live" is our promise — but it's 100% in someone else's hands. A trader who arrives at an empty shelf doesn't come back later. They just don't come back.

The model

We run our own small competitions — e.g. a $200–500 weekly round on BTC/USDT or ETH/USDT — so there's always at least one joinable round. Same machinery we're already building (rounds, leaderboard, share cards, winner posts). Zero new product work — it's a campaign config plus a budget.

The funding ladder

Now: a small own/Buddy Trading marketing allocation — buys live-product proof + community rhythm + content
Phase 2: exchange affiliate commission refills the rounds (the existing Tier-1 plan, started earlier)
Phase 2+: a slice of the partner-campaign service fee funds the shelf permanently

The hidden bonus: house campaigns are B2B lead generation

Run a small house round on TOKENX/USDT → that token's community notices their pair is live and shows up → after the round, send the token's team the results: "your community generated $X of volume on a $300 pool we funded — here's what a real campaign would do." The house campaign becomes the sales demo. We pitch issuers with their own community's results, not a deck.

To go from proposed → live, it needs: a budget + cadence decision (leadership) · first pair choice — major pair (safe) vs. target-token pair (lead-gen play) · an accounting line (marketing spend vs. future commission offset).

Section 13 · Exploration — Inspired by Cede Arena

Arena mode — 1v1 PnL duels.

Cede runs a separate product at app.cedearena.io: "PnL Trading Tournaments. Compete. Win. Be remembered." — 1v1 matches decided by PnL%, ~30-minute matchmaking. Bybit runs the same format (1–4h battles, prizes funded by the exchange as marketing spend). It looks fun because it is fun — the question is the business model.

Why it's interesting for us

PnL% = capital size doesn't matter — whale-proof by design, even more than our hourly cap. Opens the door to small traders.
Short matches = instant fun — the most gamified retention surface possible.
Can run without a B2B partner — pairs with house campaigns on the "shelf is never empty" goal.
Our architecture already reserved an "arena PvP" seam — it attaches to the same gateway and trade-ingestion rails.

The unknown: how it gets funded

Cede Arena's model isn't public. Three ways this category works elsewhere:

Sponsor-funded prizes — Bybit's model: marketing spend buys engagement
Entry stakes + winner takes the pot, platform takes a rake — self-sustaining, but gambling-adjacent → legal review required
Free duels, partner-funded rewards — Trade & Earn economics applied to matches

Before anyone gets excited: staked 1v1 on PnL needs a legal check in most jurisdictions · 1v1 PnL is gameable (two accounts, opposite positions — needs anti-abuse design) · and our MVP edge is clean Trade & Earn focus — Cede's product sprawl is the complexity we beat. Arena is Phase 3+, after the proof exists.

Next step: run our competitor-research tooling on app.cedearena.io — capture their match flow, stake/entry screens, and how prizes get funded. Decide after we know their model.
